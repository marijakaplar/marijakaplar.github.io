Hi,

I enjoy working on problems where **mathematics, computing**, and **learning** meet. My work connects mathematical thinking and formal reasoning with computational approaches and empirical research on how people learn, especially in technology-rich learning environments. One of the goals across my projects is to design and evaluate educational platforms that strengthen students’ reasoning, justification, and conceptual understanding, alongside research on detecting and characterizing students’ misconceptions in probability and statistics.

My earlier work spans the design and development of digital learning resources and platforms—from interactive materials and feedback-driven learning environments to the evaluation of learning outcomes and assessment data. More recently, my work has increasingly focused on **AI-enabled learning environments**, combining system development with empirical evaluation. Within this line of work, LLM-supported tutoring setups are designed and tested using controlled, pedagogically grounded interaction protocols (e.g., SPARK_AI). Complementary studies examine how interaction with LLMs affects learners—for example, whether misconceptions persist or change, how well incorrect model outputs are detected, and how students’ confidence in their own answers is shaped.

A significant part of my work has been dedicated to **engineering education**, where the gap between procedural fluency and conceptual understanding often becomes particularly visible. In this context, I have studied students’ reasoning and misconceptions in mathematically demanding topics and explored how instructional design and educational technologies can better support deep understanding and well-calibrated confidence.

And because my interests don’t always stay in one lane, I’ve also worked in quantitative linguistics—developing **NLP algorithms** and software for syllabification (automatic word-to-syllable segmentation), first for Serbian, and later toward a more uniform, cross-Slavic approach that can be applied across multiple Slavic languages.
